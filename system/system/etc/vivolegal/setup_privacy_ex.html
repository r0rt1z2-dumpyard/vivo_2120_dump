2) What data and information will vivo collect

① Service usage and content data

We will collect system and application data from your vivo Android device, such as your system version number, app installation behavior, and app switching behavior. Each vivo Android device is assigned with one or more unique identifiers. These identifiers are associated with your IMEI, mobile country code (MCC), and vivo Android device mobile network code (MNC, also stored by your wireless carrier) so that your device can sync services such as emails and contacts. In order to push the latest software version and fix possible faults and defects, the device hardware model and Android software version information may be collected, but not stored. We may also collect information related to device faults, such as in what location a crash occurred. To learn how users use vivo products and services so that we can further improve them to adapt to user demands and improve user experience, when you turn on the switch for the User Experience Improvement Program, we will also collect data related to your usage behaviors and service content, such as the time, frequency, and duration of accessing services, IP addresses, search terms, powering your device on/off, unlocking, screen turning on/off, usage of music, themes, wallpapers, headphones, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, camera, and cards, app installations and uninstallations, network logs, call logs, SMS logs, app lists, storage (such as recordings, photos/videos, Bluetooth data, etc.), and cookies. When the switch for the User Experience Improvement Program is disabled, we will no longer collect the above-mentioned information.

To provide targeted smart reminder and smart recommendation services, as well as make it easier for you to read key information, we will also collect the following information when Jovi Smart Scene service is enabled: travel information such as flight, train, and hotel information; express delivery tracking numbers; order information from third-party applications such as Zhixing Train Tickets, DiDi, Ctrip, Qunar, Meituan, Dianping, Taobao, JD, Taopiaopiao, Kugou Music, and Umetrip; traffic tickets, phone bills and data usage, activity data (including step counts, exercise durations, distance, location, and routes), etc. If you are not using Jovi Smart Scene, you can go to Settings > Jovi > Smart Scene to disable this service. When disabled, we will no longer collect the above-mentioned information. To increase the accuracy and efficiency of smart scene and smart recommendation services to better suit your needs, our AI engine service will also collect some information about your purchases, such as merchant information, purchase content, payment status, etc. vivo shall collect and use such information in strict accordance with national laws. During collection and use, desensitization and de-identification technologies will be applied, and we will keep such information strictly confidential and will not disclose it to any third parties. When the switch for the User Experience Improvement Program is disabled, we will no longer collect the above-mentioned information.

Third-party applications will let you choose if you want to interact and share information with vivo or other parties. If you choose to share information, vivo will collect and store your information. Before choosing to share personal information, please think carefully and proceed with caution. ④ Location data

When you request a location-based service, such as searching a specific geographic location, navigation, checking the weather, viewing the streetscape, etc., your device may send us your real-time location information (such as region ID or GPS information). We will ask you which applications you want to enable location-based services for, and remember your frequent locations to provide you easy services such as commuting reminders. You can also choose to disable location-based services on your device and not to allow location data sharing. For details about how to disable location-based services, please contact your network carrier.

⑤ Cookies and track information

Cookies are the plain text files sent from network servers to your mobile device browsers and which are stored on your mobile device. They usually include identifiers, site names, and some numbers and characters. Currently vivo only sends one kind of temporary cookies, which are set only when users enter a website by tapping a banner ad. We use such cookies to track the effect of marketing activities. Please note that you can choose to enable or disable cookies. By changing the browser preferences, you can choose to enable all cookies, require notifications for cookies, or disable all cookies. After disabling cookies, you can still use the vivo IT data network, but some website features and functions may not work properly or may even stop working altogether.
